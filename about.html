About The Lodge

A collaborative space for environmental leadership

Our Story

The Lodge represents a unique convergence of UW Law students and graduate researchers working in natural resources, environmental science, and related fields. We believe that solving today's environmental challenges requires diverse perspectives—bringing together legal expertise, scientific knowledge, and field experience.

Founded on the principle that learning happens both in the classroom and in the field, The Lodge provides opportunities for meaningful engagement with real-world environmental issues and the stakeholders working to address them.

Our Mission

🌍 Interdisciplinary Learning

We bridge the gap between law and natural sciences, fostering a deeper understanding of how policy, science, and on-the-ground action intersect in environmental management.

🏕️ Experiential Education

Through field trips and site visits, members gain direct experience with environmental challenges and solutions, from forest management practices to water resource issues to conservation efforts.

🤝 Stakeholder Engagement

We connect with foresters, land managers, farmers, conservationists, tribal representatives, and other stakeholders who are actively working on environmental challenges.

💡 Professional Development

Members develop networks, gain practical knowledge, and build skills relevant to careers in environmental law, policy, conservation, and natural resource management.

Our Field Trips

The Lodge regularly organizes field trips to diverse environmental locations and with various stakeholders:

🌲 Forest Management

Learn about forestry practices, timber management, wildfire prevention, and ecosystem restoration with local foresters and land managers.

💧 Water & Agriculture

Explore water resource challenges in agricultural regions, including irrigation practices, basin management, and competing uses.

🏞️ Conservation Lands

Visit protected areas and conservation projects to understand land acquisition, habitat restoration, and wildlife management.

🌊 Watershed & Fisheries

Study salmon recovery, river restoration, dam management, and fish habitat conservation efforts across the Pacific Northwest.

🏛️ Environmental Policy

Meet with agencies like the EPA, USFS, and state environmental departments to understand regulatory frameworks and implementation.

🌾 Tribal Stewardship

Learn from tribal nations about traditional ecological knowledge, sovereignty, and contemporary environmental leadership.

Who We Are

Bringing Diverse Perspectives Together

⚖️ UW Law Students

Students interested in environmental law, natural resources law, policy, and the intersection of legal systems with environmental challenges.

🔬 Environmental Science Graduate Students

Researchers and scholars in ecology, conservation biology, environmental science, and related fields from across the University.

🌱 Natural Resources & Forestry Students

Students from the College of the Environment and other programs working on forests, fisheries, and sustainable resource management.

🎓 Faculty & Advisors

Faculty members from various schools support and mentor the organization, bringing subject matter expertise and professional connections.
